What is Computer Forensics?

Computer forensics is the practice of collecting, analyzing, and preserving digital evidence from computers, networks, and storage devices. This evidence is often used in legal cases, internal investigations, and incident response.

{/* Added pl-8 */}

Importance of Computer Forensics

In an increasingly digital world, computer forensics plays a crucial role in uncovering the truth behind cyber incidents, intellectual property theft, fraud, and other digital crimes. It helps organizations understand what happened, mitigate damage, and prevent future occurrences.
